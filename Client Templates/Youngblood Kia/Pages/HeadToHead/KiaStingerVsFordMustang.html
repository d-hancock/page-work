2018 Kia Stinger vs. 2018 Ford Mustang: Performance

The 2018 Kia Stinger is available with either a 2.0L turbocharged four-cylinder engine or a 3.3L twin-turbocharged six-cylinder engine. The 2018 Ford Mustang is available with either a turbocharged four-cylinder or an eight-cylinder engine. In a drag race between the Kia Stinger and Ford Mustang conducted by Motoring Australia, the new Kia Stinger GT ran a quarter-mile in 12.83 seconds, compared to the 13.32 seconds needed by the Ford Mustang GT.

Performance features of the 2018 Kia Stinger include:

  • 8-speed automatic transmission
  • Limited-slip differential
  • Rear wheel drive or available all-wheel drive with an electronically controlled transfer case
  • Dynamic torque vectoring control
  • Brembo® brakes
  • High performance dampers
  • Electronic controlled suspension
  • ECO, SPORT, COMFORT, SMART, and CUSTOM driving modes

2018 Kia Stinger vs. 2018 Ford Mustang: Technology

Both the new 2018 Kia Stinger and 2018 Ford Mustang offer infotainment systems with Bluetooth®. However, the Kia Stinger comes with more standard features than the Ford Mustang, including an Android Auto™/Apple CarPlay™ interface and a 7-in. touchscreen compared to the 4.2-in. screen in the Mustang.

Available features of the 2018 Kia Stinger include:

  • Blind Spot Collision Warning with Lane Change Assist
  • Rear Cross Traffic Collision Warning
  • Forward Collision Avoidance Assist with Pedestrian Detection
  • Advanced Smart Cruise Control with Stop and Go
  • Lane Departure Warning System
  • Lane Keep Assist
  • Driver Attention Warning
