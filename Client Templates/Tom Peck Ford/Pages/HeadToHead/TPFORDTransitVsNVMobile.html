2018 Ford Transit

Starting at $35,100 MSRP, the 2018 Ford Transit makes a capable full-size passenger van affordable. Continue reading for the key features and specs of this passenger van.

Engine Specs

The 2018 Ford Transit includes a 3.7L Ti-VCT V6 engine standard, but this full-size passenger van also offers two other engine options. Each engine is powerful enough to ensure a safe and smooth daily drive. The Transit also includes a six-speed automatic overdrive with SelectShift® transmission to truly help pack a punch on the road. Listed below are the key engine specs of all three engine options the Transit has to offer.

Exterior

With a clean-lined exterior, the 2018 Ford Transit provides a stylish passenger van without sacrificing on any of the necessities. The power, short-arm mirror is standard, but also includes optional heated short arm mirrors with power folding and turn signals, long arm power mirrors, and heated, power long arm mirrors with turn signals. Other exterior features of the transit include:

  • Halogen headlamps with chrome tip and AutoLamp®
  • Rain Sensing Wipers
  • Roof marker lamps
  • 60/40 hinged passenger side doors
  • Black body side moldings

Interior

With a comfy interior, the 2018 Ford Transit will appeal to everyone. With black, plastic step well pads and a passenger pillar grab handle, the Transit ensures the ease and comfort of your passengers. Inside this full-size passenger van are pewter grey cloth two-way manual driver and passenger seats standard, but it also includes seven optional seat customizations to best suit your needs. The seating options available for the 2018 Transit include:

  • Pewter grey, leather-trimmed front seats with ten-way power driver and passenger
  • Charcoal front cloth seats with two-way manual driver and passenger
  • Pewter grey front cloth seats with ten-way power driver and two-way manual passenger
  • Charcoal front cloth seats with ten-way power driver and two-way manual passenger
  • Pewter grey cloth front seats with ten –way power driver and passenger
  • Charcoal front cloth seats with ten-way power driver and passenger
  • Charcoal, leather-trimmed front seats with ten-way power driver and passenger

Safety and Technology

The 2018 Ford Transit is dedicated to keeping you and your passengers safe by including high-tech features such as lane keeping alert with driver alert and a SOS Post-Crash Alert System™. The Transit also uses technology features like an AM/FM stereo with audio input jack and 4-in. multi-function display. If the standard technology features are not enough for you, the Transit also offers four other optional entertainment systems. The 2018 Transit optional technology features include:

  • Audio system with AM/FM stereo with audio input jack, 4-in. multi-function display, single-CD, and SiriusXM® capability
  • Audio system with AM/FM stereo with audio input jack, 4-in. multi-function display, Message Center, and SYNC® with USB port
  • Audio system with AM/FM stereo, 4-in. multi-function display, Message Center, SYNC® with USB port, single-CD, HD and SiriusXM® radio capability
  • Audio system with AM/FM stereo, SYNC® 3 with navigation, 6.5-in. color, multi-function display media hub, HD and SiriusXM® radio capability

2018 Nissan NV

The 2018 Nissan NV starts at $37,120 MSRP and provides some of the features of the 2018 Ford Transit for slightly more money. Continue reading for key features of the Nissan NV.

Engine Specs

The 2018 Nissan NV comes standard with the 4.0L DOHC V6 engine, but does not offer the option to choose your engine. The Nissan NV contains an engine with 261 HP and 281 lb.-ft. of torque. The Ford Transit has a standard 3.7L Ti-VCT V6 engine with more horsepower at 275 HP.

Exterior

The 2018 Nissan NV does not include optional outside mirror options and only includes standard dual power outside mirrors. This passenger van does include privacy glass throughout the entire vehicle and a chrome grille and bumpers.

Interior

Offering standard grey cloth front seats with reinforced side bolster wear patches and water-repellant seating surfaces standard, the 2018 Nissan NV does not provide optional seating options. This passenger van offers an eight-way power adjustable driver’s seat and four-way manual passenger seat. The NV does offer 324 seating configurations, but it does not offer a passenger pillar grab handle or step well pads.

Safety and Technology

The 5-in. color display AM/FM/CD audio system comes standard with the 2018 Nissan NV and offers an optional 5.8-in. color display. This passenger van also includes technology features like a three month trial of SiriusXM® and Bluetooth® streaming audio. For safety features, the NV includes vehicle dynamic control with traction control system and zone body construction with front and rear crumple zones.

The Winner

The clear winner of this comparison is the 2018 Ford Transit. It offers more on the inside and outside for a better price than the 2018 Nissan NV. The Transit is also more customizable than the NV, allowing you to create your passenger van in a way that fits your needs best.
