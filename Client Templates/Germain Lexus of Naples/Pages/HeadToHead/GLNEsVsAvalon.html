2019 Lexus ES vs. 2019 Toyota Avalon: Specs and Performance

The 2019 Lexus ES and 2019 Toyota Avalon are built on the same new platform, which lowers the center of gravity for improved handling, and use the same 3.5L V6 engine. Both are also available as a hybrid as well. However, the Lexus ES is just slightly more powerful and fuel-efficient with either powertrain.

The Lexus ES is also available with F SPORT performance tuning, such as an adaptive variable suspension and Sport S+ drive mode. And unlike the Toyota Avalon, every new Lexus ES is equipped with paddle shifters.

2019 Lexus ES vs. 2019 Toyota Avalon: Interior Comfort

The cabin of the Toyota Avalon is a comfortable place to spend your commute, thanks to standard features like dual-zone climate control and available features like heated and ventilated front seats. However, the Lexus ES has all these features plus several more.

  • The Avalon is available with a power tilt/slide moonroof with a sunshade, and the Lexus ES with a panoramic glass roof.
  • The Lexus ES provides the best rear legroom in its class.
  • The Avalon has an 8-way power-adjustable driver’s seat with up to 4-way lumbar adjustment. Lexus ES owners can enjoy a 14-way power-adjustable driver’s seat with a power-extendable seat cushion for added thigh support.
  • Only the Lexus ES is available with a power rear sunshade.
  • Only the Lexus ES is available with a heated wood and leather-trimmed steering wheel.
  • The semi-aniline perforated leather upholstery in the Lexus ES is of higher quality than the leather used in the Toyota Avalon.

2019 Lexus ES vs. 2019 Toyota Avalon: Technology

The infotainment systems in the Lexus ES and Toyota Avalon are incredibly versatile, including everything from app suites to cloud-based navigation updates to on-board Wi-Fi hotspots. Both are also available with wireless smartphone charging. That said, the Lexus ES has more impressive technology overall.

  • The Lexus ES is available with a 12.3-in. multimedia and navigation display with a touchpad controller that recognizes pinch and swipe gestures as well as handwritten input. The Toyota Avalon uses a 9-in. touchscreen.
  • Although the Toyota Avalon is available with a Head-Up Display, the 10.2-in. version in the new Lexus ES is larger and more impressive.
  • Only the Lexus ES comes with Amazon Alexa integration.
  • Lexus Enform Destination Assist provides 24-hr access to a live response center agent to help you locate addresses and nearby points of interest.
  • The 17-speaker, 1,800-watt Mark Levinson® PurePlay sound system available in the new Lexus ES is unlike anything offered in a vehicle before.
  • The Lexus Safety System+ 2.0 contains all features in the Toyota Safety Sense™ P suite of safety equipment, plus Lane Tracing Assist and Road Sign Assist.
