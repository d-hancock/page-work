2019 Lexus GX vs. 2018 Mercedes-Benz GLS: Interior

Lexus and Mercedes-Benz offer some of the most luxurious interiors in the industry. You can be sure you’ll never find fault with either of these models in terms of comfort. That said, the Lexus GX 460 Luxury is more comfortable, and uses higher-end materials than the Mercedes-Benz GLS 450.

  • The GX has standard leather upholstery, while GLS uses synthetic leather
  • The GX has three-zone climate control, while the GLS has single-zone
  • Both have heated front seats but GX also has ventilated front seats
  • Both have 10-way power driver’s seats, and power-folding third row seats, but only the GX is available with second-row captain’s chairs
  • The GX comes with a wood and leather trimmed heated steering wheel

2019 Lexus GX vs. 2018 Mercedes-Benz GLS: Safety

Most new SUVs come with safety features like advanced airbags, tire pressure monitors, and backup cameras. Luxury SUVs like the Lexus GX and Mercedes-Benz GLS go even further and add features like rain-sensing windshield wipers, radar cruise control, and lane departure alert. The Lexus GX 460 Luxury comes standard with several additional features that are either not available or only available for an additional cost on the Mercedes-Benz GLS. These include:

  • LED headlights, fog lights, and taillights
  • Headlight washers
  • Automatic high beams
  • Blind spot monitor with rear cross traffic assist
  • Lexus Enform Safety Connect emergency, roadside, and stolen vehicle assistance

2019 Lexus GX vs. 2018 Mercedes-Benz GLS: Technology

Some of the most impressive vehicle technology currently available can be found in the new Lexus GX and Mercedes-Benz GLS. For example, both offer large multimedia displays with built-in navigation, dual-screen rear entertainment systems, and impressive sound systems. However, the GX proves to be more impressive in many ways.

  • The Lexus navigation system includes 3D city views, simulated views of approaching highway junctions, and more
  • Lexus Enform Destination Assist provides 24-hour access to a live response center agent to help you locate addresses and nearby points of interest like restaurants
  • With Lexus Enform Remote, GX owners can remotely start the engine and climate control system from their smartphone, smart watch, or Amazon Alexa™ unit
  • Although the Mercedes-Benz model is compatible with SiriusXM Traffic and Weather services, the Lexus GX comes with subscription-free traffic and weather updates, as well as Fuel, Sports, and Stocks apps
  • Mercedes-Benz has a 13-speaker harman/kardon® premium sound system, while Lexus has a 17-speaker Mark Levinson® premium sound system
