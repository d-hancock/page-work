Every new Nissan Murano comes equipped with a tough V6 engine, and while the Ford Edge has three engine options, most use a Turbo I4. The Nissan is more powerful, slightly more efficient, and more fun to drive than the Ford Edge.

2018 Nissan Murano vs. 2018 Ford Edge: Interior

Both the Nissan Murano and Ford Edge are equipped with popular features like standard push-button start and an available panoramic sunroof, but the Nissan Murano goes much further to satisfy passengers than the Edge.

  • A heated steering wheel, heated and cooled front seats, and heated rear seats are standard on upper trim packages of the Murano, but are still an added-cost option on even the top tier trim of the Ford Edge.
  • The Ford Edge comes standard with a 4.2-in. central display screen while the Nissan Murano comes standard with an 8-in. central display screen, Apple CarPlay™/Android Auto™ integration, and a navigation system.
  • The Nissan Murano comes standard with dual-zone automatic climate control plus rear air vents, but the Ford Edge does not.
  • The Nissan Murano comes standard with multiple USB ports, including one for rear passengers.

2018 Nissan Murano vs. 2018 Ford Edge: Safety Features

Do you want a safe vehicle? Then the choice is obvious; the 2018 Nissan Murano earned the top score possible in every crash safety test conducted by the IIHS, and earned a “superior” rating for its crash avoidance technology. Meanwhile, the 2018 Ford Edge did not score as well in the crash tests and earned only a “basic” rating for its own crash avoidance technology.

Features available in the Nissan Murano but not the Ford Edge include:

  • Standard Intelligent Forward Collision Warning
  • Standard Automatic Emergency Braking
  • Intelligent Around View® Monitor
  • Driver Attention Monitor

2018 Nissan Murano vs. 2018 Ford Edge: 5-Year Ownership Costs

The experts at Edmunds.com created the True Cost to Own® tool to help shoppers learn how much a certain car may cost over a five year period, assuming the owner drives 15,000 miles per year. Although the Nissan Murano starts at $30,800 MSRP and the Ford Edge starts at $29,315 MSRP, the Ford Edge doesn’t hold its value nearly as well, and the Nissan Murano’s superior reliability and fuel economy mean it is much more affordable in the long run.
