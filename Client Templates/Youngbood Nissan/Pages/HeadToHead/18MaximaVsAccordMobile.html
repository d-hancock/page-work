2018 Nissan Maxima vs. 2018 Honda Accord: Interior Features

You will be comfortable in both the Nissan Maxima and Honda Accord, thanks to standard features like dual-zone automatic climate control and available features like heated and cooled front seats. But if you truly want to feel pampered, you’ll prefer the 2018 Nissan Maxima.

  • While the Honda Accord is available with leather seating, the Nissan Maxima provides a more luxurious experience with available premium ascot leather seats with diamond-quilted inserts.
  • The Nissan Maxima comes standard with an 8-way power-adjustable driver’s seat while the Honda Accord’s driver’s seat only has manual height adjustment.
  • Although the Honda Accord does have an adjustable steering column, only the Nissan Maxima has a power-tilt-and-telescoping steering column.
  • The remote start feature of the Nissan Maxima allows you to start the car and get the air or heat going before getting inside or even leaving the house!
  • While the Honda Accord is available with a sunroof, the Nissan Maxima has a dual-pane panoramic sunroof that both rows of passengers can enjoy.

2018 Nissan Maxima vs. 2018 Honda Accord: Safety & Technology

You will have a hard time finding fault with the safety features of either of these models. After all, both come standard with a forward collision warning and automatic emergency braking and offer a blind spot and rear cross-traffic monitor. Looking over all technology options, however, it’s clear that the Nissan Maxima has so much more to offer than the Honda Accord.

  • The Nissan Maxima comes standard with twice as many speakers as the Honda Accord, and offers Active Noise Cancellation and Active Sound Enhancement.
  • The Nissan Maxima has four USB ports to the Honda Accord’s two.
  • The Nissan Maxima has an 8-in. touchscreen display, larger than the one in the Honda Accord, and a 7-in. full-color cluster display.
  • Apple CarPlay™/Android Auto™ and hands-free text messaging are standard on the new Nissan Maxima, but not on the Honda Accord.
  • While both the Honda Accord and Nissan Maxima come with a standard rearview monitor, only the Maxima is available with an Intelligent Around View® monitor.
