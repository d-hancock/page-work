Synthetic Oil vs. Conventional Oil

What is synthetic oil and how does it differ from conventional engine oil? Synthetic oil has fewer impurities and contains additives to improve performance. Synthetic oil is more resistant to extreme temperatures than conventional oil, more effective at reducing deposits of dirt and grime, and more effective at lubricating engine components.

So which should you use at your next oil change? Synthetic oil is better for your car. Synthetic oil can also improve performance, potentially extend the life of high-mileage cars, and flows better in cold temperatures. Conventional oil is perfectly acceptable in most cars, and is a more cost-effective option. Oil Change Intervals

Most auto service technicians recommend changing your engine oil and oil filter every six months or every 5,000 miles, whichever comes first. If you frequently tow with your vehicle, or if the majority of your trips are under 5 miles, in stop-and-go traffic, or in extreme heat or cold, then your engine oil will break down faster and need to be changed every 3,000 miles.
