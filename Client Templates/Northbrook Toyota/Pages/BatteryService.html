Diagnosing a Dead Battery

If you suspect that your car battery is failing, stop into the service center at Northbrook Toyota today for a voltage test. We will diagnose whether your vehicle is suffering from a dead battery, a faulty starter, or bad alternator. However, these seven signs are a good start to diagnosing a bad battery.

  1. Engine starts slowly, inconsistently, or not all
  2. No interior lights when you attempt to start the car
  3. You’ve jumped your car three times or more in the same week
  4. A battery warning light is illuminated on your dashboard when running
  5. Battery case appears swollen on a visual inspection
  6. Battery terminals appear corroded on a visual inspection
  7. It’s been more than three years since you last replaced the battery

Battery Replacement

Car batteries won’t last forever, so it’s inevitable that you will need to replace your car battery a few times throughout your vehicle’s life. Most auto technicians recommend replacing your car battery every three years, although you can check your owner’s manual to see the exact recommendation for your specifific model and year.
